Both models required the same number of revision loops (3), meaning haiku matched sonnet's quality trajectory at 4× lower cost and 2.7× lower latency on this topic.

For high-volume content pipelines where the reviewer loop self-corrects quality, haiku presents a compelling cost-performance tradeoff — with sonnet reserved for tasks where first-draft quality is critical.
